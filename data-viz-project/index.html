How Income and Ideology Are Shaping Parents' Views on K–12 Education in 2025

Parent survey findings | March 2025

By Madison Eades

As education continues to be highly contentious in the 2024 presidential election’s aftermath, new polling reveals a growing divide among school parents. Despite income differences, many parents feel K-12 education is on the 'wrong track', and trust in the Department of Education is fracturing. The survey, conducted in early 2025, captures shifting public opinion as education remains a top political battleground nationwide.
